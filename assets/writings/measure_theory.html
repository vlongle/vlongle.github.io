1.1 Sigma Algebra
In measure theory, we want to assign a notion of ”size” called measure (think
length, area, volume) to each “nice” object. The simplest kind of thing to

For a more complicated shape like circle, we can still use Archimedes’s idea of
embedding a polygon inside a circle. We know how to compute the area of a
polygon: we just break it into a bunch of triangles, whose areas we know how
to compute, and add them up afterwards. As we increase the polygon’s sides,
its area better and better approximates the circle’s area.
