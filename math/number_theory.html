1.2 Prime Factorization (Season 1: Existence)
Prime numbers are numbers that can be divided by itself and 1. They are
the atoms in mathematics, the building blocks of numbers. ”Atom” means,
in Greek, ”indivisible”, which ﬁts the deﬁnition of prime.
We show that every number can be reduced to multiplication of primes
Proof by contradiction:
Suppose we have a number n that is not prime (If n is prime then there is
nothing to prove), we assert that n is not product of primes. We make a
stronger statement, n cannot be divided by any prime that is smaller than n.
Since n is not prime, it must have at least two factors (Yes prime does have
two factors: itself and 1, but normally when we talk about factors, we ignore
1). Call the smallest factor of n, a. a must be prime, since if it’s not, then
it has some factors and therefore is not the smallest factor. Ignore this prime
factor a, since n is composite (meaning non-prime), it still has some factors.
Now, say b is the next smallest factor, by the same argument b must be prime.
Proceeding in this manner, we see that we basically chop down all the factors
of n into primes and only PRIMES!
